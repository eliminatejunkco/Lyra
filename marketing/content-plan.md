## 📸 Master Photo Guide — What to Shoot & How

Great photos are the single biggest factor in getting more calls from social media. Here is exactly what to photograph and how to do it.

### Types of Photos to Take on Every Job

| Photo Type | When to Shoot | What It Shows |
|---|---|---|
| **Before** | Arrive on site before you touch anything | The messy reality — makes viewers say "I have that too" |
| **After** | Before you leave, space swept clean | The transformation — triggers the urge to call |
| **During (load)** | Truck/trailer being loaded | Shows scale of the work, proves you handle big jobs |
| **Full truck** | Truck completely loaded, parked on street | Visual proof of volume — great for estimating cost |
| **Team on the job** | You or your crew carrying items | Human face on the business — builds trust |
| **Single item spotlight** | One interesting item before removal | Hooks viewers who have the same item at home |
| **Clean sweep close-up** | Broom, swept floor, empty space | Detail shot that signals quality and thoroughness |

### How to Take a Good Before/After on Your Phone

1. **Stand in the same exact spot** for before and after — the side-by-side comparison is the most powerful post you can make.
2. **Shoot horizontal (landscape)** — it fills Facebook and Nextdoor feeds better.
3. **Back up to show the whole space** — do not crop items out of frame.
4. **Shoot toward natural light** (window or open garage door) so it is bright and clear.
5. **No need to edit** — real and raw looks authentic on social media.

### When You Don't Have a Photo

Use this backup order:
1. A stock-style shot of your truck parked on a clean street with your name/number visible
2. A photo of you smiling next to your empty truck after a big job
3. A short video (15–30 seconds) of you doing a walk-through of an empty garage or cleared room

## 📌 Platform Notes

### Facebook Groups
- Join 5–10 local groups (Buy Nothing, Community, Neighborhood, Homeowners, Moms groups)
- Post the same caption in each group — just slightly vary the city name in the first line
- Best posting times: **7–9 AM** or **6–8 PM**
- Always reply to every comment within 15 minutes — first reply usually wins the job
- Use 2–4 hashtags per post: `#JunkRemoval #InlandEmpire #[CityName] #EliminateJunkRemoval`

### Nextdoor
- Nextdoor does **not** allow hashtags — skip them here
- Write in a **neighbor-to-neighbor tone** — less salesy, more personal
- Mention your specific service area (neighborhood or city name) in the post
- Best posting times: **8–10 AM Tuesday or Saturday**
- Respond to comments publicly so other neighbors can see the interaction
