## 1. Craigslist (Free — Post Daily)

Craigslist is still one of the highest-traffic sources for junk removal leads. Many competitors don't post consistently, so staying active gives you an edge.

### How to Post

2. Click **Post to Classifieds** → Services → Household
3. Post a new ad **every 2–3 days** (older ads get buried — renew or repost regularly)

## 2. Thumbtack (Pay-Per-Quote)

Thumbtack connects service pros with local customers actively seeking quotes. You only pay when you quote a job.

### How to Set Up
1. Create a free profile at [thumbtack.com/pro](https://thumbtack.com/pro)
2. Categories to select: **Junk Removal**, **Furniture Removal**, **Garage Cleanout**
3. Set your service area to all Inland Empire cities
4. Upload 5+ photos of completed jobs
5. Write a strong bio (use the Google Business description as a starting point)

### Tips to Win on Thumbtack
- **Respond to leads instantly** — customers often hire the first pro to respond
- **Enable Instant Match** — lets customers book you without you sending a quote first
- Set your quote to be competitive but not the cheapest (customers trust mid-range pricing)
- Ask every Thumbtack customer for a review after the job

### Expected Cost: $5–$25 per quoted lead. Worth it if you're closing 30–40% of quotes.

## 3. Angi (formerly Angie's List) / HomeAdvisor

Both are part of the same network. You pay for matched leads in your service area.

### How to Set Up
1. Sign up at [pro.angi.com](https://pro.angi.com)
2. Select Junk Removal and all Inland Empire zip codes
3. Set a monthly budget of $50–$150 to start

### Tips
- Complete your profile 100% (photo, bio, services, certifications)
- Collect Angi reviews — they're shown to all incoming leads
- Respond to every lead within 5 minutes — Angi penalizes slow response in rankings

## 4. TaskRabbit

TaskRabbit is great for **furniture removal and single-item pickups** — jobs that larger haulers often pass on.

### How to Set Up
1. Apply at [taskrabbit.com/become-a-tasker](https://www.taskrabbit.com/become-a-tasker)
2. Select: **Furniture Assembly & Removal**, **Moving Help**, **Heavy Lifting**
3. Set your rate and available hours
4. There is a one-time registration fee (~$25)

### Tips
- Great for quick single-item furniture and appliance pickups
- Can upsell to larger cleanouts once on-site
- Keep your rating high — 5-star Taskers get more job offers

## 5. Yelp for Business

Many people search Yelp specifically for local services. A free Yelp profile gets you visible in these searches.

### How to Set Up
1. Claim your profile at [biz.yelp.com](https://biz.yelp.com)
2. Add photos, services, and business hours
3. Respond to all reviews (positive and negative)

### Tips
- Do NOT offer incentives for Yelp reviews — Yelp will penalize your listing
- After each job, say: *"If you're happy with the work, feel free to leave us a Yelp review!"*
- Yelp's free profile is enough to start — skip paid advertising until you have 10+ reviews

## 6. Nextdoor Business Page (Free)

In addition to posting in neighborhoods as a resident (covered in the [Facebook/Nextdoor playbook](facebook-posts.md)), you can set up a **free business page** on Nextdoor.

### How to Set Up
1. Go to [nextdoor.com/business](https://nextdoor.com/business)
2. Create a free Business Page for Eliminate Junk Removal
3. Add services, photos, and contact info
4. Collect **Neighbor Recommendations** — these are like verified reviews from locals

### Why This Matters
Nextdoor recommendations are extremely trusted — they come from verified neighbors, not anonymous strangers. Even 5–10 recommendations can generate consistent calls from your immediate service area.

## 7. OfferUp & Facebook Marketplace

Post on OfferUp and Facebook Marketplace under the **Services** section (not just items for sale).

- OfferUp: [offerup.com](https://offerup.com) → Post → Service
- Facebook Marketplace: Marketplace → Create Listing → Service

Use the same Craigslist ad copy above. Refresh the listing every few days.

## 8. Patch.com & Local Community Blogs

Many cities have a local news/community blog (Patch, local Facebook pages, etc.). Reach out to offer:
